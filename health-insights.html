Your Guide to Understanding BMI

What is BMI?

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a simple index of weight-for-height that is commonly used to classify underweight, normal weight, overweight, and obesity in adults.

BMI Categories

Underweight (BMI < 18.5):

You may need to gain some weight for better health. Focus on balanced nutrition with higher calorie intake and strength training exercises.

Normal Weight (18.5 – 24.9):

Great! Keep up your healthy habits with regular physical activity and a balanced diet.

Overweight (25 – 29.9):

Consider adopting healthier lifestyle choices like reducing sugar, eating more vegetables, and staying active.

Obese (BMI 30+):

Talk to a healthcare professional about a safe plan to reduce weight. Include more physical activity and focus on portion control and nutritious food.

Note

BMI is a useful tool but not a perfect measure of health. It doesn’t consider muscle mass, bone density, and overall body composition.
